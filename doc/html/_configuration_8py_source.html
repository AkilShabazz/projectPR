5 # This program is a neuronal simulation system, intended for studying spinal cord neuronal
6 # networks responsible for muscle control. These networks are affected by descending drive,
7 # afferent drive, and electrical nerve stimulation. The simulator may be used to investigate
8 # phenomena at several levels of organization, e.g., at the neuronal membrane level or at
9 # the whole muscle behavior level (e.g., muscle force generation). This versatility is due
10 # to the fact that each element (neurons, synapses, muscle fibers) has its own specific
11 # mathematical model, usually involving the action of voltage- or neurotransmitter-dependent
12 # ionic channels. The simulator should be helpful in activities such as interpretation of
13 # results obtained from neurophysiological experiments in humans or mammals, proposal of
14 # hypothesis or testing models or theories on neuronal dynamics or neuronal network processing,
15 # validation of experimental protocols, and teaching neurophysiology.
16 #
17 # The elements that take part in the system belong to the following classes: motoneurons,
18 # muscle fibers (electrical activity and force generation), Renshaw cells, Ia inhibitory
19 # interneurons, Ib inhibitory interneurons, Ia and Ib afferents. The neurons are interconnected
20 # by chemical synapses, which can be exhibit depression or facilitation.
21 #
22 # The system simulates the following nuclei involved in flexion and extension of the human or
23 # cat ankle: Medial Gastrocnemius (MG), Lateral Gastrocnemius (LG), Soleus (SOL), and Tibialis
24 # Anterior (TA).
25 #
26 # A web-based version can be found in [remoto.leb.usp.br](http://remoto.leb.usp.br/remoto/index.html).
27 # The version to which this documentation refers is from a Python program that can be found in
28 # [github.com/rnwatanabe/projectPR](https://github.com/rnwatanabe/projectPR).
